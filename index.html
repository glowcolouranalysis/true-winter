The simple rule: If it looks warm, muted, dusty, or earthy — skip it. If it looks cool, clear, crisp, and high-contrast — it belongs to you. Think: "Cool first. Clear second."

This is a guide, not a rulebook. Every colour in your palette can be worn anywhere. Clothing, makeup, nail polish, jewellery, eyewear, ties, hats, scarves and beyond. Use this as a reference to understand what works with your natural colouring, then trust yourself. Colour analysis is about empowerment, not restriction.

Your palette is limitless. The True Winter palette contains thousands of colours. The swatches in this guide are carefully chosen examples and reference points to help you understand the qualities to look for — cool, clear, and high contrast. When you are shopping, use these as a visual compass, not a checklist. If a colour shares those same qualities, it likely belongs to you.

Screen tip: For the most accurate colour reference, turn off your blue light filter and set your screen brightness to around 80 percent or higher before browsing your palette. On iPhone, look for Night Shift under Settings > Display & Brightness. On Samsung, look for Eye Comfort Shield under Settings > Display. On other Android devices it may be called Night Light or Blue Light Filter under Settings > Display.

Online shopping tip: When shopping online and unsure about a colour, try asking an AI assistant with the brand, product, and colour name included. For example: "Is [Brand] [Product] in [Colour Name] cool or warm toned? Does it suit True Winter?" Then do a visual check by comparing the item against your palette. Use AI as a starting point. It has limitations and pulls from what is available online, so always check samples in store when possible, contact the brand directly, or ask staff for help.

Accessories & Eyewear
Your palette is your guide for every accessory — jewellery, bags, shoes, scarves, eyewear, ties, hats, nail polish and more.
The bigger picture: Every colour in your palette can be referenced for accessories. A True Winter red bag, an icy blue scarf, a deep burgundy nail — it all works. The guidance below helps you understand why certain choices feel more harmonious, so you can shop with confidence rather than follow a checklist.

Black frames are your standout choice — they echo your natural contrast and look effortlessly sharp. Navy, charcoal, and silver are equally strong. For a more expressive look, deep plum or dark teal frames are stunning. Avoid: warm tortoiseshell, golden brown, amber, or orange-toned frames.
Lens tints for sunglasses
Cool grey, blue-grey, or mirrored silver lenses are your most harmonious options. Green-tinted lenses also work well. Avoid amber, brown, or warm orange lenses — they cast a warm light that works against your colouring. Mirrored lenses in silver or blue are a great statement choice for True Winter.

Black is your most powerful and versatile base. Silver and white are striking neutrals. A cool dark chocolate or espresso brown works beautifully for bags and shoes — look for shades that lean cool and dark with no warm red or orange undertone. Avoid: warm tan, camel, beige, or warm brown.
Scarves, Hats & Wraps
Near-face accessories matter most
Anything worn close to your face — scarves, hat brims, collars — has the same impact as a top or blouse. Use your Near The Face colours as a guide here. An icy lavender scarf, a winter red hat, or a cobalt wrap near your face will all be flattering. Tip: a cool-toned scarf near the face can transform an otherwise neutral outfit into something that truly works for your season.

Glitter and chrome finishes in silver or icy tones look incredible on True Winter. Avoid: warm nude, peach, coral, orange, or brown-toned polishes.
For the Men
Ties, pocket squares & accessories
The same palette applies. Your True Winter colours translate directly into ties, pocket squares, cufflinks, and accessories. A winter red or royal blue tie is a classic power choice. Deep navy, charcoal, and black suits are your strongest base. Silver cufflinks over gold. A cool white shirt over cream. A cobalt or burgundy tie over brown or olive.
Beard & grooming colour
If you colour your beard or brows, Just For Men is a reliable go-to. Look for the cooler or darker shades such as black, soft black, or dark brown. Avoid anything with a warm, reddish, or auburn tone. When in doubt, go one shade darker rather than lighter, and always do a strand test first. Tip: the same cool and ash tone guidance applies here as it does for hair colour.
Makeup & Hair
Your True Winter guide to beauty. Cool, clear, and high contrast. Shades listed are longstanding classics worth looking for.
Your golden rule: If it looks warm, peachy, golden, or brown-toned — put it back. True Winter makeup is cool, vivid, and high contrast. A bold cool lip with minimal eye makeup will always look more "you" than a full warm face. Less is more, but make it count.
Lips
Your most powerful beauty tool. Blue-based reds and cool berries are your naturals — they mimic your own colouring and look effortless rather than "done." Red is your power colour here: it signals confidence, passion, and presence.

Deep cool plums and blue-burgundies are stunning for evenings. Avoid warm plums with brown or red-brown undertones — look for cool, jewel-toned depth instead.
Your "nude" lip — cool, not beige
True nudes don't work for you — they wash you out. Your version of a "no-makeup lip" is a cool pink, soft raspberry, or sheer berry balm. Look for shades described as cool rose, cool mauve, or icy pink. Try: ILIA Balmy Tint in Naked, RMS Beauty Wild Roses worn lightly, or a cool rose lip liner worn alone.
Blush
Cool pinks and soft berries bring life to your face without fighting your natural colouring. Blush should look like a cool flush of colour — not warmth. Skip bronzer entirely.

Look for cool pink, cool rose, or berry-toned blush. Avoid peach, coral, bronze, or terracotta. Tip: a multi-stick used on lips and cheeks is a beautifully cohesive approach.
Foundation & Tinted Moisturiser
Your most important foundation rule: cool or neutral undertone only. Pink-based or neutral foundations work best. Yellow or golden-based foundations will fight your natural colouring.
What to look for
Ask for pink, cool, or neutral undertone in your shade. Avoid shades described as golden, warm, yellow, or honey. For tinted moisturisers, ILIA Skin Tint and RMS Beauty "Un" Cover-Up are great natural options. Tip: when testing foundation on your wrist, a match that disappears means neutral. One that looks orange or yellow means it's too warm for you.
Eyes
Your eyes thrive with cool contrast and definition. Frosted and metallic finishes catch the light beautifully on you. Skip the brown palette and reach for cool greys, silvers, and icy tones instead.
Eyeshadow — what works
Base shades: icy white, cool grey, silver, soft lavender. Depth: charcoal, graphite, black, deep navy, cool plum. Accents: icy blue, icy lilac, electric violet, cool teal. Frosted and metallic finishes are your friend. Avoid: gold, bronze, warm brown, terracotta, peach, or any warm metallic.
Eyeliner
Black is your most powerful liner. If pure black feels too stark, try cool graphite, dark navy, or deep charcoal. Avoid brown liner, which softens the very contrast that makes you striking. Tip: a thin precise black liner with a cool berry lip is a quintessential True Winter look.
Mascara
Black mascara is your best friend. Dark navy mascara works too. Brown or grey mascara will dilute your natural intensity. Try: Physician Formula Butter Mascara in Black, NYX Worth the Hype, or ILIA Fullest Volumizing Mascara.
Brows
Brow guidance
Match your brow product to your natural hair colour, or go one shade darker. If you have dark hair, a cool dark brown or soft black brow product works beautifully. If your hair is lighter, opt for a cool taupe or cool ash brown — never a warm or golden brown. Tip: ask for "cool" or "ash" tones when shopping brow pencils or powders.
Hair Colour
Your most beautiful hair colour is often the one you were born with. True Winter's natural colouring tends toward deep, cool, high-contrast hair — and that is exactly what flatters you most.
Your best hair colours
Deep and cool: blue-black, cool espresso, cool dark brown (level 5 or deeper). Light options: platinum blonde or silver — these are striking on True Winter and the only light shades that truly work. Avoid: golden blonde, ash blonde, warm auburn, copper, honey brown, caramel, or highlights — these pull warm and create tension with your natural cool clarity.
Embracing silver & grey
Silver and grey hair can be absolutely stunning on True Winter. It mirrors the icy, high-contrast quality of your palette beautifully. If you are going silver naturally, consider leaning into it rather than covering it. Tip: a purple or blue toning shampoo keeps it cool and bright rather than yellow.
What to ask your colourist
Ask for "cool toned," "no warmth," or "level 5 or deeper" for darker shades. Show your colourist your palette — having your actual cool colour reference makes the conversation so much easier and avoids misunderstandings. Tip: when in doubt, go deeper and cooler rather than lighter and warmer.
