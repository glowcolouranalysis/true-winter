The simple rule: If it looks warm, muted, dusty, or earthy skip it. If it looks cool, clear, crisp, and high-contrast it belongs to you. Think: "Cool first. Clear second."

This is a guide, not a rulebook. Every colour in your palette can be worn anywhere. Clothing, makeup, nail polish, jewellery, eyewear, ties, hats, scarves and beyond. Use this as a reference to understand what works with your natural colouring, then trust yourself. Colour analysis is about empowerment, not restriction.

Your palette is limitless. The True Winter palette contains thousands of colours. The swatches in this guide are carefully chosen examples and reference points to help you understand the qualities to look for — cool, clear, and high contrast. When you are shopping, use these as a visual compass, not a checklist. If a colour shares those same qualities, it likely belongs to you.

Screen tip: For the most accurate colour reference, turn off your blue light filter and set your screen brightness to around 80 percent or higher before browsing your palette. On iPhone, look for Night Shift under Settings > Display & Brightness. On Samsung, look for Eye Comfort Shield under Settings > Display. On other Android devices it may be called Night Light or Blue Light Filter under Settings > Display.

Online shopping tip: When shopping online and unsure about a colour, try asking an AI assistant with the brand, product, and colour name included. For example: "Is [Brand] [Product] in [Colour Name] cool or warm toned? Does it suit True Winter?" Then do a visual check by comparing the item against your palette. Use AI as a starting point. It has limitations and pulls from what is available online, so always check samples in store when possible, contact the brand directly, or ask staff for help.

Accessories & Eyewear
Your palette is your guide for every accessory jewellery, bags, shoes, scarves, eyewear, ties, hats, nail polish and more. Use these as starting points, not rules.
The bigger picture: Every colour in your palette can be referenced for accessories. A True Winter red bag, an icy blue scarf, a deep burgundy nail it all works. The guidance below helps you understand why certain choices feel more harmonious, so you can shop with confidence rather than follow a checklist.

Black frames are your standout choice they echo your natural contrast and look effortlessly sharp. Navy, charcoal, and silver are equally strong. For a more expressive look, deep plum or dark teal frames are stunning. Clear or translucent frames work well if they have a cool blue or grey tint. Avoid: warm tortoiseshell, golden brown, amber, or orange-toned frames these fight your cool clarity.
Lens tints for sunglasses
Cool grey, blue-grey, or mirrored silver lenses are your most harmonious options. Green-tinted lenses also work well. Avoid amber, brown, or warm orange lenses they cast a warm light that works against your colouring. Mirrored lenses in silver or blue are a great statement choice for True Winter.

Scarves, Hats & Wraps
Near-face accessories matter most
Anything worn close to your face scarves, hat brims, collars has the same impact as a top or blouse. Use your Near The Face colours as a guide here. An icy lavender scarf, a winter red hat, or a cobalt wrap near your face will all be flattering. Tip: a cool-toned scarf near the face can transform an otherwise neutral outfit into something that truly works for your season.

For the Men
Ties, pocket squares & accessories
The same palette applies. Your True Winter colours translate directly into ties, pocket squares, cufflinks, and accessories. A winter red or royal blue tie is a classic power choice. Deep navy, charcoal, and black suits are your strongest base. Silver cufflinks over gold. A cool white shirt over cream. A cobalt or burgundy tie over brown or olive. The contrast and cool clarity that defines True Winter shows up beautifully in menswear.
Beard & grooming colour
If you colour your beard or brows, Just For Men is a reliable go-to. Look for the cooler or darker shades such as black, soft black, or dark brown. Avoid anything with a warm, reddish, or auburn tone as these will fight your natural cool colouring. When in doubt, go one shade darker rather than lighter, and always do a strand test first. Tip: the same cool and ash tone guidance applies here as it does for hair colour.
Makeup & Hair
Your True Winter guide to beauty. Cool, clear, and high contrast. Shades listed are longstanding classics worth looking for.
Your golden rule: If it looks warm, peachy, golden, or brown-toned put it back. True Winter makeup is cool, vivid, and high contrast. A bold cool lip with minimal eye makeup will always look more "you" than a full warm face. Less is more, but make it count.
Lips
Your most powerful beauty tool. Blue-based reds and cool berries are your naturals they mimic your own colouring and look effortless rather than "done." Red is your power colour here: it signals confidence, passion, and presence. Berry and fuchsia communicate creativity and individuality. Even worn sheer, a cool lip elevates your whole look.

Your "nude" lip cool, not beige
True nudes don't work for you they wash you out. Your version of a "no-makeup lip" is a cool pink, soft raspberry, or sheer berry balm. Look for shades described as cool rose, cool mauve, or icy pink. Try: ILIA Balmy Tint in Naked, RMS Beauty Wild Roses worn lightly, or a cool rose lip liner worn alone.
Blush
Cool pinks and soft berries bring life to your face without fighting your natural colouring. Blush should look like a cool flush of colour not warmth. Skip bronzer entirely; it will fight your cool clarity and make your skin look muddy rather than glowing.

Foundation & Tinted Moisturiser
Your most important foundation rule: cool or neutral undertone only. Pink-based or neutral foundations work best. Yellow or golden-based foundations will fight your natural colouring. When in doubt, go neutral over warm.
What to look for
Ask for pink, cool, or neutral undertone in your shade. Avoid shades described as golden, warm, yellow, or honey. For tinted moisturisers, ILIA Skin Tint and RMS Beauty "Un" Cover-Up are great natural options request your shade in the cool or neutral range. Physician Formula and Marcelle both have accessible drugstore options look for their lightest-coverage formulas in cool undertones. Tip: when testing foundation on your wrist, a match that disappears means neutral. One that looks orange or yellow means it's too warm for you.
Eyes
Your eyes thrive with cool contrast and definition. Frosted and metallic finishes catch the light beautifully on you. Matte warm neutrals dull your natural intensity skip the brown palette and reach for cool greys, silvers, and icy tones instead.
Eyeshadow what works
Base shades: icy white, cool grey, silver, soft lavender. Depth: charcoal, graphite, black, deep navy, cool plum. Accents: icy blue, icy lilac, electric violet, cool teal. Frosted and metallic finishes are your friend they add dimension without warmth. Avoid: gold, bronze, warm brown, terracotta, peach, or any warm metallic.
Eyeliner
Black is your most powerful liner it enhances your natural contrast beautifully. If pure black feels too stark for you, try cool graphite, dark navy, or deep charcoal. Avoid brown liner, which softens the very contrast that makes you striking. Tip: a thin precise black liner with a cool berry lip is a quintessential True Winter look.
Mascara
Black mascara is your best friend it enhances the contrast between your eyes and skin that is so distinctive to True Winter. Dark navy mascara works too. Brown or grey mascara will dilute your natural intensity. Try: any lengthening black mascara Physician Formula Butter Mascara in Black, NYX Worth the Hype, or ILIA Fullest Volumizing Mascara.
Brows
Brow guidance
Match your brow product to your natural hair colour, or go one shade darker. If you have dark hair, a cool dark brown or soft black brow product works beautifully. If your hair is lighter, opt for a cool taupe or cool ash brown, never a warm or golden brown. Defined brows enhance the clean, graphic quality of True Winter's best features. Tip: ask for "cool" or "ash" tones when shopping brow pencils or powders. Just For Men also makes a great brow and beard product for filling in sparse areas, look for the cooler or darker shades and avoid anything with a warm or reddish tone.
Hair Colour
Your most beautiful hair colour is often the one you were born with. True Winter's natural colouring tends toward deep, cool, high-contrast hair and that is exactly what flatters you most. If you colour your hair, the goal is to honour that natural depth and coolness rather than fight it.
Your best hair colours
Deep and cool: blue-black, cool espresso, cool dark brown (level 5 or deeper) rich and cool without red or auburn warmth. A natural-looking chocolate brown with a slightly warm overtone can also be beautiful, as long as it doesn't pull red or orange. Light options: platinum blonde or silver these are striking on True Winter and the only light shades that truly work. Avoid: golden blonde, ash blonde, warm auburn, copper, honey brown, caramel, or highlights these pull warm and create tension with your natural cool clarity.
Embracing silver & grey
Silver and grey hair can be absolutely stunning on True Winter. It mirrors the icy, high-contrast quality of your palette beautifully. If you are going silver naturally, consider leaning into it rather than covering it. A cool silver or steel grey is one of the most flattering things a True Winter can wear on their head. Tip: if transitioning to silver, a purple or blue toning shampoo keeps it cool and bright rather than yellow. The same trick works for cool brown hair, a blue or purple toning shampoo used occasionally helps neutralise any brassiness and keeps your colour looking crisp and cool.
What to ask your colourist
Ask for "cool toned," "no warmth," or "level 5 or deeper" for darker shades. If going platinum or silver, ask for a toner that neutralises any brass or gold. Show your colourist your palette having your actual cool colour reference makes the conversation so much easier and avoids misunderstandings. Tip: the goal is always to support your natural colouring, not fight it. When in doubt, go deeper and cooler rather than lighter and warmer.

Ask it a few different ways for the best result and trust your eye.
